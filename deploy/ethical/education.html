Debt Trap Statistics

  • 75% of payday loan revenue comes from borrowers trapped in 10+ loans per year
  • Average borrower is in debt 5 months out of the year
  • Typical borrower pays more in fees than they originally borrowed

📊 Understanding APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

What is APR?

APR is the yearly cost of a loan, including interest and fees, expressed as a percentage. It helps you compare the true cost of different loans.

How Payday Loan APR is Calculated

Example: $100 loan with $15 fee for 14 days
Step 1: Cost of credit = $15
Step 2: Cost ÷ Principal = $15 ÷ $100 = 0.15 (15%)
Step 3: Annualize = 0.15 × (365 ÷ 14) = 3.91 (391%)
Result: 391% APR

APR Comparison

Credit Card 15-30%
Personal Loan 6-36%
Auto Loan 3-12%

💡 Better Alternatives to Payday Loans

🏦 Credit Union PAL (Payday Alternative Loan)

Best Option

APR: 18-28% maximum

Amount: $200-$1,000

Requirements: Credit union membership (often easy to get)

Benefits: Much lower cost, helps build credit

💳 Credit Card Cash Advance

Often Better

APR: 25-30% typical

Amount: Varies by credit limit

Requirements: Existing credit card

Benefits: Lower cost, no rollover fees

🤝 Employer Advance

Often Free

Cost: Often no fee or small fee

Amount: Usually limited to earned wages

Requirements: Employer must offer program

Benefits: Very low or no cost

👥 Borrow from Family/Friends

Usually Free

Cost: No interest if agreed

Amount: Depends on relationship

Requirements: Trust and clear agreement

Benefits: No fees, flexible terms

🏢 Payment Plan with Creditor

Often Free

Cost: Often no additional fee

Amount: Based on debt owed

Requirements: Contact creditor directly

Benefits: Avoids additional debt

🆘 Emergency Assistance Programs

Free

Cost: Free

Amount: Varies by program

Requirements: Income qualification often required

Benefits: No repayment needed
